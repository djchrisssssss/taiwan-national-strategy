## Notes

- Section 14.1 (Controlled Nuclear Fusion) builds upon Section 5.4's discussion of nuclear fission (SMRs) as a medium-term strategy, extending the energy independence roadmap to the long-term fusion horizon (2040-2060+).
- Section 14.2 (Long-Distance Energy Transmission) complements Section 5.5 (Microgrids and Distributed Energy) by addressing inter-grid and international energy interconnection possibilities.
- Section 14.3 (National-Level Blockchain Network) extends Chapter 8's treatment of blockchain as a financial backup instrument into a comprehensive sovereign digital infrastructure vision.
- Section 14.4 (UAP Investigation) follows the institutional model established by the US AARO (established 2022 under DoD/DNI) and NASA's UAP Independent Study Team (report published September 2023). The section treats UAP investigation as a matter of airspace sovereignty, sensor capability, and scientific inquiry — consistent with the analytical framework used throughout this document.
- The contact preparation subsection within 14.4 applies the same low-probability/extremely-high-consequence scenario analysis framework used elsewhere in this document (e.g., full maritime blockade scenarios in Chapter 5), treating the topic with institutional seriousness rather than speculation.
